### Considered Datasets 
> #### Considered Dataset 1:
>> - Name: Ocular Disease Recognition
>> - Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/andrewmvd/ocular-disease-recognition-odir5k
>> - Objective 1: To predict a diagnosis of various eye defects via multi label classification.
>> - Objective 2: To predict eye diseases from the images of the retina via multi class classification

> ##### Considered Dataset 2:
>> - Name: Heart Disease Dataset
>> - Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/johnsmith88/heart-disease-dataset
>> - Objective 1: Finding patient clusters based on gathered information such as blood sugar levels, resting blood pressure etc.
>> - Objective 2: Classifying a patient's disease based on available information
>> - Objective 3: Finding the feature with the highest colleration with the presence of heart disease in a patient

>> - #### Considered Dataset 3
>> -  Name: Medical Diagnosis Data
>> -  Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/uom190346a/disease-symptoms-and-patient-profile-dataset#:~:text=calendar_view_week-,Disease_symptom_and_patient_profile_dataset,-.csv
>> - Objective 1:To analyze how disease prevalence varies across different age groups and genders.
>> - Objective 2:What are the correlations between different health features (blood pressure and cholesterol levels)? Do certain symptoms tend to occur together?
>> - Objective 3:Using visualization tools like histograms, scatter plots and bar chats to better understand the relationships and patterns in the data.

> ##### Considered Dataset 4
>> - Name: Diabetes Disease Dataset
>> - Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/jameshramos/pima-indians-diabetes
>> - Objective 1: To analyze whether a patient has diabetes based on their health attributes.
>> - Objective 2: Categorizing individuals into different risk groups for diabetes based on their health attribute.
>> - Objective 3: To visualize the risk of diabetes based on age group and compare and analyze diabetes risk between males and females.
